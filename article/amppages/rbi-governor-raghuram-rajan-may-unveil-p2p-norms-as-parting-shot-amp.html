RBI Governor Raghuram Rajan may unveil P2P norms as parting shot

Updated on: Apr 04, 2017 42:30 pm IST
Media Centre

Source: ET Bureau, economictimes.com

As Reserve Bank of India governor Raghuram Rajan prepares to deliver what would be his last monetary policy speech on Tuesday, speculations are rife that he will announce regulations for peer-to-peer (P2P) lending. 
P2P lending - a booming online service globally where individuals and businesses borrow directly from lenders, or investors, with the help of P2P platforms that play matchmakers - is currently unregulated in the country. 

Industry insiders said the central bank is working overtime to ensure that before Rajan demits office on September 4, key steps in the direction of a fullfledged regulation for the fledgling sector can be released. 

In April, RBI had come out with a consultation paper on P2P lending platforms and asked for public suggestions on the need to regulate them. Next step for the regulator is to come out with draft guidelines and ask for further suggestions before coming out with the real regulations. 

Industry sources said there is a high chance that Rajan would mention P2P regulation in his Tuesday's speech, followed by the release of the final draft paper by midAugust. 
"The two major hindrances holding up the regulations were regarding legal issues around the Moneylending Act and intricacies of payments procedure. As far as we know, they have been mostly addressed. Hence, the regulations should be out any time," said one of the founders of a P2P lending platform who did not wish to be identified.
